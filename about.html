Our Mission

In a rapidly evolving world, where traditional sources of knowledge often fall short, this website emerges as a beacon for those seeking enlightenment through alternative means. Founded by two autodidacts, shaped by the shortcomings of conventional education, and fueled by an insatiable curiosity, it represents a collective awakening. As Alvin Toffler once noted, “The illiterate of the 21st century will not be those who cannot read and write, but those who cannot learn, unlearn, and relearn.”

Once pillars of knowledge and guidance, the traditional family and religion have seen their influence wane in an era of unprecedented access to information. The transformation mirrors Toffler’s vision of a new kind of literacy in this century.

Our experiences in community college, marred by a lack of genuine learning opportunities, have contributed to the growing disillusionment with academia we share with many of our fellow Americans. John Dewey once said: “Education is not preparation for life; education is life itself.” This disconnect between education and the visceral realities of everyday life highlights the systemic failures in these traditional institutions and further advances the shift in American learning, where more people choose YouTube, Reddit, Google, and social media as their preferred medium.

And in the realm of journalism, the shift from objective reporting to a more biased and sensationalist approach is hard to deny.

Our journey led us to the internet, a vast repository of unfiltered information. Inspired by Francis Bacon’s words, “Knowledge is power,” we embraced the digital world as a means to educate ourselves, breaking free from the constraints of traditional learning.

While the internet opened doors to self-guided learning, it also brought challenges. More than ever, our need for discernment and critical thinking is crucial to how we develop as individuals and a society. Like Socrates once said: “I cannot teach anybody anything, I can only make them think.”

In this landscape, our website stands as a testament to the power of shared, self-acquired knowledge. Echoing Nelson Mandela’s belief, “Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world,” we are committed to fostering a community of learners who seek to empower themselves and others through open dialogue and exploration.

This platform is more than just a repository of information; it is a movement, a shift away from passive consumption of knowledge to active, engaged learning. In the spirit of the great teachers and learners of the past, we embrace education as our tool, not just to understand the world, but to change it.
